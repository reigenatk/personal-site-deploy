I've kinda forgotten how to blog.. it's been a year plus 3 months. I had a post in the works for last summer, but it was too cringe to upload.. actually I think I did upload it but I took it down.

I have a habit of getting too personal in these posts, maybe because I don't have many people to tell it to, but when I write online I can be as detailed as I want. Kinda like how people on tumblr will post really long stories about their lives, under an anonymous username. Except here, I can't hide behind a moniker.

This blog post will be pretty disorganized. I'll just address the things that come to mind.

I've moved..

to the Bay Area. This will be my first time living alone, like truly alone. I've always at least lived with some kind of family. And in many ways, I used that as an excuse to pardon some of the laziness.. and also used it as an excuse to blame others for my shortcomings. Oh, I can't go to this event because my dad won't approve.. or my brother is a control freak and he ruined this or that.. etc.

But now that I own a car and I'm thousands of miles from any kind of authority, it's solely my fault if I'm not happy. In other words, I feel like I've been given the wheel of the ship and I can finally steer it wherever I please. But that also means I have to start taking blame for my own misery. A double-edged sword kind of feeling.

me when pirate

I sat on it for a while, pondering whether it would be worth it to bring this site up again.. And then I decided it wasn't worth the effort.

Until yesterday... when I had a change of heart. Why did I bother?

Well: I realized it's one of the last bastions of my creative self. A place where I can be more than just a nameless person. A canvas to be who I really am, through writing.

I believe every person should express themselves through some form of art. Maybe its the romantic side of me speaking. But I think that art is the rawest form of being human, and it's an amazing thing to create it. It brings color to an otherwise boring, sad life. Imagine if the only thing people talked about all day were money and business and other formalities. What a dull world it would be.

I've gotten into this weird hobby recently of collecting pictures that invoke some kind of emotional resposne in me. Maybe I'll make an entire page of these images.

What does this image make you feel? To me it's the feeling of being incredibly warm. While also being trapped, giving a slight feeling of terror. I feel like someone's coming up the stairwell.

This is why you see me making so many random things without any reason or cause.. Shitty videos, shitty music, shitty games, shitty art, you name it. And there will be more to come. There's a creative spirit inside of me. And I feel the urge to let it out, just like you would to go to the bathroom and take a dump after a bad meal.

I want to be more than just another nerd making 120k in San Jose, living to 70-odd years or however long the average life expectancy is, maybe starting a family, and then passing away.. No, that's just lame to me. That's a life not worth living. As silly as it sounds, being a reckless creative seems way more enticing. The people who amaze me the most are the one who wakeup everyday, so passionate about something that all they can think of is doing that one thing. That's exactly what I, and so many others in this world, lack.

I want to leave something behind, something that others, even people whom I never knew, can find. And they can go: "Oh, this person existed and he was like [so and so]." And that would be enough for me. I don't need to be famous. I just want to be different.

I don't know. Maybe I'll eat my words and live the most average life this earth has ever seen. That would be funny, wouldn't it? I guess my definiton of success would be to get reasonably good at some form of art, enough to the point where I can look back on it and say that I'm proud of it.

I've made a resolution to spend at least 1 hour of my busy schedule to do something creative, no matter what.

A robotic life

And that's precisely why I made this game last semester, which I called "A robotic life". The name comes from the r9k board on 4chan, which is known to be full of bitter incels. I spent a little too much time on that board, (lurking not posting), and I found it very relatable to my own life.

Specifically, the fact that most people on that board have been outcasts their entire lives, and have reached the conclusion that they are fundamentally different from most humans, whom they affectionately dub as "normies". They call each other "robots" and have a whole slew of names that they used to refer to different sub-categories of "normies". Now it might sound like I take this stuff real seriously, but I don't. I just think its hilarious, while also being a somewhat scarily accurate representation of the world we live in.

I took away some inspiration from the memes on the board, and decided to make a game. And at that time, I was playing lots of indie RPG games like Oneshot, LISA, OMORI, etc.

My game "logo" screen.. I couldn't come up with anything fancy

I get really defensive about this game when anyone mentions it because.. it's embarassing. It's a raw depiction of how I see my own life. The "robot", that's who I am at the core. A misfit. A person who doesn't feel strongly about anything, but how screwed up and different he is compared to everyone else around him.

And it's also a very buggy game with lots of cringe dialogue and code that may or may not work (honestly I didn't have the heart to keep playingtesting it.. turns out developing a game is hard.)

I didn't even record a good playthrough of it for my Youtube channel.. although the trailer itself is alright.

My voice recording is all buzzy and loud, which could've been easily fixable had I just checked before recording the gameplay. Honestly the whole project is a mess and that's why I'm embarassed to share it. I don't have the heart to re-record it. I haven't even tried promoting it anywhere (my original plan was to upload it to Steam, but no one would ever pay for such a flaming pile of trash). Which sucks, because I spent six months making it. I had planned to spend months, years- whatever it took to make the game I wanted. But I can say with certainty that I burned myself out pretty hard, pretty quickly. On paper, it was a huge waste of time.

But the funny thing is, as I said before, I felt like I had to make it. Something was calling me to. And because of that, I don't regret putting in the time to create it.

To this day, it's the most creative thing I've ever done. And perhaps the plan from the start was flawed- like, who is going to put in the time to play a 2-hour long game from some random person? Nobody, of course.

But through making the game, I got to make cool pixel art (lots of which I didn't know I was capable of making- I'm quite proud of how accurate lots of the sprites are to their originals), compose original soundtracks (I'm quite happy with most of the tracks.. I feel like they express a part of me through music, which makes me happy.), and learn some Unity programming of course, lots of which was very rewarding to figure out (integrating Yarn Spinner, working with Unity Timeline, and event systems come to mind).

I mean damn, look at this pepe!

gawd damn

Even today, having been 6 months out from my last work on the project (I stopped working around New Years of 2023), I feel lots of warmth when I look back on the time I spent recklessly working towards my indie game dream. The goal was never to be famous. Well, maybe it was.. I always admired people like Toby Fox or Eric Barone who could make a one-hit wonder game and receive tons of fame for it. But that's obviously not something everyone can do. I wanted to be a game developer for a while, too, until I realized how competitive the industry is and how stressful the job is. But deep down, I was mostly trying to create something I would be proud of. Maybe five years down the line I can look back on the game and have a laugh about the person who I was when making it.

It's funny because there's not a lot of things that can capture my attention like that in life. But I'm grateful that it could do that for me. It's on my itch.io if you want to look at it.

Shorter projects?

So that's my main point established. I want to remain someone who creates things. And that's why I hate work, since it drains the energy that inspires one to create. The reason why I even bother maintaining the "favorites" page here is to jot down what inspires me and what motivates me to make things.

Since no one (including me) will sit down to play such a long game (I don't even want to open my own game, that's why I recorded the gameplay, because I knew even I wouldn't want to look at it after finishing it), I decided that shorter art forms may be worth exploring. I think authors often suffer from this too, reading a book is a huge time investment, and most people will just give up reading your book. Music is a good example, but even a 3 minute song is a tall ask for some random person to listen to.

I've been looking into webcomics and animations since they are easily consumable while being just as effective of a medium to express oneself. Webcomics like Stonetoss (although he is very controversial) and other similar ones are cool. Though I wouldn't touch politics with a 10 foot pole.. my subjects would be less provocative.

I have also always been amazed with Alan Becker, Don Hertzfeldt, and offshoot small youtube channels like I can't sleep. Oh, and Killer Bean. Can you comprehend that one guy made that? He even showed his setup that he used to make it, there were tons of computer guts (GPUs) just laid out on a table with like fans whirring and everything. Check this out (timestamped):

IDK why but that's so inspiring. This man has GPUs that are pretty ass by today's standard, running software that looked like it was made in 1990s, nowhere even close to as robust as Unreal Engine or Maya today. And yet he was still able to make a masterpiece. And just by looking at the guy, you wouldn't be able to tell. He just look like an average dude.
